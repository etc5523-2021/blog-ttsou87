Introduction

As we move towards the third quarter of 2021, many countries around the world have been accelerating their vaccination process. Unfortunately, the same cannot be said for other countries, especially those developing countries without the technology or resource to develop their own vaccines. Nigeria, being a country under the Humanitarian Response Plan (HRP), is one of the countries that requires external support. In this blog post, I will be looking into the coronavirus situation in Nigeria using data from Our World in Data.

Data Description

Two data sets are used in this short overview of COVID-19 situation in Nigeria, one on COVID-19 cases and one regarding the vaccination progress in Nigeria Hannah Ritchie and Roser (2020).

COVID-19 Confirmed cases and Death

Due to the conditions in Nigeria, many of the more detailed variables such as icu patients, weekly_icu_admissions..etc. are missing from the data set. As COVID-19 related data is aggregated for countries all around the world, more developed countries with better recording resources will have more data. Nigeria, on the other hand, lacks record hence it has 45 less columns of data in comparison. For the purpose of my analysis, I cleaned up the data to remove the columns with completely no data, and used the remaining columns to illustrate the conditions of Nigeria.

Vaccine Administered

The following table contains the data descriptions for the vaccination progress:

Table 3: Data Description table for the vaccination progress
Variables Description
date Date of the observation
total_vaccinations Total number of doses administered. For vaccines that require multiple doses, each individual dose is counted. If a person receives one dose of the vaccine, this metric goes up by 1. If they receive a second dose, it goes up by 1 again. If they receive a third/booster dose, it goes up by 1 again.
people_vaccinated total number of people who received at least one vaccine dose. If a person receives the first dose of a 2-dose vaccine, this metric goes up by 1. If they receive the second dose, the metric stays the same.
people_fully_vaccinated Total number of people who received all doses prescribed by the vaccination protocol. If a person receives the first dose of a 2-dose vaccine, this metric stays the same. If they receive the second dose, the metric goes up by 1.
daily_vaccinations_raw Daily change in the total number of doses administered. It is only calculated for consecutive days.
daily_vaccinations New doses administered per day (7-day smoothed).
